Mustangs come back to beat Gladiators at home.

24-Nov-2024

AMSTERDAM, The Netherlands -- The Amsterdam Mustangs snatched a 2-1 victory from the Leiden Gladiators in a thrilling game on home ice. The first period saw both teams battling for control, with three penalties called but no goals scored. After a scoreless first period, The Gladiators struck first in the second period, with Emilio Harris-Mostert deflecting a high wrist shot from Mark Gasseling into the net. The Mustangs were awarded a penalty shot after Gasseling's interference penalty, however Jared Penner was unable to convert.

The Mustangs' bench had some early difficulties managing their lines early in the game, but adjustments in the 3rd period allowed the team to regain control. The Mustangs showed resilience, coming back in the third period with two goals to secure the victory. Jared Penner got his vengeance and tied the game for the Mustangs off a deflected pass from Krisztian Vizsy. The game-winning goal later came from Petteri Hyytiäinen on a power play with Daniel Watts assisting. Petteri's shot deflected off a defender into the Gladiators' net past a frustrated Vincent Regis.

The Mustangs capitalized on one of their three power play opportunities, while successfully killing off all three penalties they took. The Gladiators, on the other hand, were unable to convert on any of their three power plays.

Tori Holmes-Kirk, the Mustangs' goaltender, made crucial saves throughout the game, especially during a late surge by the Gladiators, to secure the win. This victory highlights the Amsterdam Mustang's ability to overcome adversity and secure a win on home ice.

This was a key victory for the Mustangs over their Leiden rivals. The Gladiators and Mustangs often battle to control the upper half of their division. The Mustangs move to third place in the North division but face another tough challenge on December 1st in a re-match in an away game versus Groningen.
