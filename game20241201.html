Mustangs fall short in Groningen.

01-Dec-2024

GRONINGEN, The Netherlands -- The Amsterdam Mustangs fell short against a physical Groningen team, losing 10-5. Despite a strong start and a third-period surge, the Mustangs were unable to counter Groningen in a hard-fought match.

The game was marked by numerous penalties. The referees struggled to control the game, leading to a chippy atmosphere throughout. Mustangs’ player Jim Conboy received multiple penalties, including two for checking from behind and a misconduct penalty. This hampered the Mustangs' efforts, going down to three defensemen and contributed to Groningen's offensive success.

Although Jeremie Gould opened the scoring for the Mustangs just 30 seconds into the game with a goal from behind the net, Groningen quickly answered with three consecutive goals, setting the tone for most of the match. Starting the 3rd period down 7-2, the Mustangs got three quick goals at the start of the period to get the score to 7-5. However, that momentum didn’t continue until the end of the game.

Edwin Pijl was a key player for Groningen, scoring a hat trick and adding two assists. For the Mustangs, Jeremie Gould had a goal and an assist, while Petteri Hyytiäinen contributed two assists. On special teams, the Mustangs continue to excel on penalty kill but need to finish better on their power play opportunities.

This was a tough defeat for the Mustangs as they were hoping to split their series with Groningen. For the time being, the Mustangs maintain third place in the North division and will face a struggling Heerenveen team at home in 2 weeks.
