Mustangs fight to the end but fall short in home opener.

03-Nov-2024

AMSTERDAM, The Netherlands -- The Amsterdam Mustangs lost a hard-fought battle in their home opener against Groningen, falling 7-6 in a game that saw both sides rack up penalties. Despite a nail-biter that saw them tie the game multiple times and pull the goalie in the final minutes, the Mustangs couldn't tie up the game with the extra attacker.

The game was a back-and-forth affair. Groningen opened the scoring and took a 2-0 lead in the first period. The Mustangs answered with a power-play goal from Jim Conboy and a goal from Daniel Watts to even the score. From that point, the game was always close with Groningen either leading or the game being tied throughout. The Mustangs continuously battled back but were never able to take the lead. Groningen regained the lead before the end of the first period, making it 3-2. The second period was won by the Mustangs and the game was tied after two periods. The third period followed a similar pattern, with Groningen ultimately scoring the game-winning goal on a power play with under six minutes remaining.

Daniel Watts led the Mustangs with two goals, including one short-handed. Jim Conboy scored a power-play goal, while Jeremie Gould, Duane Mattos, and Jared Penner each added a goal. Gould managed to jam in a partially-covered puck before the whistle that generated complaints from Groningen. Groningen's offense was fueled by the Ukrainian duo of Nikita Murtyshchev and Maxim Kuzmenko, as well as Marcel Altepost. Murtyshchev had two goals and two assists, Kuzmenko had a goal and an assist, and Altepost scored twice and added an assist.

Both teams struggled with penalties, with the Mustangs taking 10 penalty minutes. Groningen found itself in the box for 14 penalty minutes. Jeroen Valentijn and Jared Penner each took multiple penalties for the Mustangs as did Edwin Pijl for Groningen.

Mustangs’ goalie Tori Holmes-Kirk had a strong game, making several key saves, including stopping a penalty shot by Edwin Pijl.

The Mustangs will look to rebound from this loss when they travel to face Hoorn on November 9th. Blue Mountain Cougars Hoorn 3 is currently struggling in second-to-last place with a minus 22 goal differential, providing an opportunity for the Mustangs to get back on track.
